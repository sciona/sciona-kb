# Kalmanmeasurementupdate

> Pure functional Kalman predict-and-update kernel. Consumes the current immutable state tuple (P, Q, R, X) together with a scalar measurement and produces a brand-new state tuple (P_new, X_new). Internally fuses the time-update (predict) step — advancing P by Q — with the measurement-update step that computes the Kalman gain, corrects the state estimate X, and contracts P by the complement of the gain. Returns new objects; the input state is never mutated.

## Conceptual Summary

ScalarGaussianPriorObservationFuser. Accepts a prior scalar estimate and its associated uncertainty (covariance), advances the uncertainty forward in time by additive process-noise, then computes an optimal convex-combination weight (the gain) from the ratio of prior uncertainty to total uncertainty (prior + observation noise). Uses that gain to blend the prior estimate toward the scalar observation and to contract the uncertainty by the complement of the gain — yielding a posterior estimate that is the minimum-variance unbiased fusion of two competing Gaussian beliefs. The entire operation is a pure function: all inputs are read-only and a new (posterior-estimate, posterior-uncertainty) pair is returned.. Applications: Geophysical sensor fusion: continuously refining an estimate of subsurface temperature or pressure at a fixed depth by blending a slow-drift model (Q) with noisy point-sensor readings (R), producing a running posterior depth-state used by autonomous drilling controllers., Epidemiological prevalence tracking: maintaining a running scalar estimate of the true infection rate in a population where each new aggregate test-batch yields a noisy count; Q captures day-to-day biological drift and R captures sampling variability, producing a posterior prevalence used to trigger intervention thresholds., Adaptive signal denoising in telecommunications: estimating the true amplitude of a slowly varying carrier envelope from a stream of noisy sampled observations, where Q models the envelope's random walk and R models receiver noise, enabling real-time gain-control with minimal lag., Robotic proprioception calibration: tracking the latent scalar bias-drift of an inertial measurement unit (e.g., gyroscope zero-offset) by fusing a Brownian-motion drift model with intermittent zero-velocity calibration pulses, producing a continuously corrected bias estimate that feeds downstream attitude computation.

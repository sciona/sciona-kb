# Optimalexecutiontrajectory

> Solves the Almgren-Chriss discrete-time optimal liquidation problem: given total shares and a horizon in days, applies dynamic-programming / optimal-control to produce the cost-minimizing share-selling schedule that balances market-impact cost against price-risk, parameterised by risk_aversion.

## Conceptual Summary

ConstrainedResourceDepletionOptimizer. Given a fixed scalar quantity to be fully consumed across a finite sequence of discrete time steps, applies backward-induction dynamic programming to compute the cost-minimizing depletion schedule. The optimization balances two competing convex penalty functionals: a flow-rate-dependent execution cost (penalizing large incremental reductions per step) against an inventory-dependent variance cost (penalizing slow depletion of the remaining quantity), governed by a scalar trade-off coefficient. The result is a monotonically non-increasing state trajectory from the full initial quantity to zero, where each intermediate value represents the globally optimal residual quantity to hold at that time step.. Applications: Radiotherapy dose fractionation scheduling: given a total ionizing-radiation dose to be delivered to a tumor over a fixed number of treatment sessions, find the per-session dose sequence that minimizes cumulative healthy-tissue damage (a rate-dependent cost) while controlling residual tumor-burden variance across the horizon, Battery discharge trajectory optimization: given a fixed stored-energy quantity in an electrochemical cell to be fully discharged over T discrete intervals, compute the current-draw schedule that minimizes resistive heating losses (convex in draw rate) while penalizing large residual charge at intermediate steps to reduce capacity fade risk, Bandwidth-constrained data pipeline draining: given a fixed-size data buffer to be fully transmitted across T scheduling slots, determine the per-slot transmission-volume sequence that minimizes total congestion-penalty cost (convex in instantaneous throughput) while trading off against the variance of queue backlog over time, Pharmaceutical dissolution rate planning: given a fixed drug payload in a controlled-release scaffold to be fully eluted over T biological time windows, derive the per-window elution quantity that minimizes tissue-toxicity cost (convex in instantaneous concentration spike) while penalizing slow clearance of residual payload relative to a target pharmacokinetic profile

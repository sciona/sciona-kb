# Quantumsolutionextractor

> Post-processes the raw measurement count distribution to decode, rank, and filter the top-k bitstring solutions corresponding to valid independent sets (or QUBO ground states), mapping them back to the original graph node labelling.

## Conceptual Summary

FrequencyRankedTopKDecoder. Transforms a raw empirical frequency distribution over a discrete combinatorial symbol space (bitstrings) into a ranked, filtered, and semantically re-labeled subset of the k most probable feasible configurations. The operation applies a validity predicate to prune infeasible configurations, sorts survivors by descending observation frequency, and then applies an index-relabeling bijection to map internal symbolic indices back to the original external identifier namespace. The result is a lossy compression of the full distribution that retains only the highest-frequency, constraint-satisfying points, annotated with their original structural context.. Applications: Genomics / variant calling: given a multiset of observed read alignment patterns (bitstrings encoding SNP presence/absence across loci), extract the top-k most frequently observed haplotype configurations that satisfy biological feasibility constraints (e.g., Hardy-Weinberg consistency), remapping internal read indices back to chromosome position labels, Combinatorial optimization / scheduling: after stochastic local search or simulated annealing produces a histogram of visited assignment vectors, decode and rank the top-k feasible schedules (satisfying resource or precedence constraints) and re-label internal slot indices back to human-readable job/machine identifiers, Natural language processing / beam decoding: given a count distribution over discrete token-sequence samples from a language model, filter sequences satisfying a structural grammar predicate, rank by empirical frequency, and remap internal vocabulary integer IDs back to surface-form string tokens for the top-k outputs, Sensor fusion / fault detection: given a distribution of binary system-state snapshots (each bit encoding a sensor's alarm status) collected over a monitoring window, extract the k most frequently occurring fault-pattern configurations that match known failure-mode signatures, remapping bit positions back to physical sensor identifiers in the engineering schematic

# Temperingfactorcomputation

> Computes a deterministic tempering multiplier across sub-steps (with bounds checking) to scale the transition strength.

## Conceptual Summary

IndexedTransitionGainSchedule. Maps a global intensity parameter and a discrete sub-step index to a deterministic scalar gain used to modulate update magnitude within a multi-step process. The computation enforces index validity against the total step count, yielding a well-defined scaling factor for every admissible sub-step. This is a pure schedule evaluation: no persistent state is mutated, and output is fully determined by the provided inputs.. Applications: Adaptive learning-rate scaling across inner iterations of numerical optimization in scientific computing, Stepwise trust-region or damping control in nonlinear equation solvers for computational physics, Scheduled actuation gain modulation across micro-steps in robotic trajectory execution, Progressive regularization-strength scheduling over sub-epochs in neural network training

# Template_extraction

> Extract individual heartbeat waveform templates around each R-peak with configurable before/after windows.

## Conceptual Summary

Event-Triggered Signal Epoching. Segments a 1D numerical series into a collection of fixed-length subsequences (epochs). Each subsequence is centered around a corresponding index provided in a separate list of 'event' markers. This operation effectively transforms a continuous signal into a stacked 2D array of discrete, event-aligned segments, while filtering out event markers that are too close to the signal's boundaries to form a complete epoch.. Applications: Geophysics: Extracting seismic P-wave and S-wave arrival waveforms from a continuous seismogram based on automatically detected arrival times., Finance: Isolating asset price fluctuations from a time-series immediately following discrete market events, such as earnings announcements or policy changes., Neuroscience: Aligning and averaging Event-Related Potentials (ERPs) from continuous EEG data, time-locked to the onset of external stimuli., Astrophysics: Segmenting light curve data from a star around periodic transit events (e.g., exoplanet transits or pulsar signals) to stack and enhance signal-to-noise ratio.

# Compute_aerodynamic_force

> Compute aerodynamic drag force from velocity.

## Conceptual Summary

signed_nonlinear_resistive_mapping. Transforms a signed scalar rate into a signed scalar opposing term whose magnitude increases with the rate magnitude (typically second-order in absolute value). The mapping is memoryless and applies an odd-symmetric dissipation law: zero input yields zero output, and reversing input sign reverses output sign while preserving magnitude profile.. Applications: Rotational system modeling: map angular velocity to opposing damping torque in actuator or flywheel simulations, Electromechanical modeling: map current or speed to counteracting loss term for energy dissipation estimation, Biomechanical dynamics: map joint segment velocity to passive resistive force in simplified limb models, Fluid transport networks: map signed flow rate to opposing pressure-drop term in reduced-order pipeline/channel models

# LANL Earthquake Prediction - 1st Place (2019) - Pipeline Template

## Problem

Signal processing / Regression solution template for LANL Earthquake Prediction - 1st Place (2019), modeled as a 6-stage deterministic pipeline: Signal chunking, Time-domain features, Frequency-domain features, Adversarial validation, and downstream competition-specific post-processing.

## Approach

This template captures the ordered steps used by LANL Earthquake Prediction - 1st Place (2019): prepare the data, build task-specific representations, train or apply the main model, and convert outputs into the final competition submission.

## CDG Walkthrough

{/* <ScionaViewerPlaceholder cdgId="solution.kaggle.lanl_earthquake_1st" readOnly={true} /> */}

1. **Signal chunking** -- This stage slice the continuous 1D acoustic data into fixed-size chunks of 150,000 readings (numpy).
1. **Time-domain features** -- This stage calculate mean, standard deviation, quantiles, and rolling peak counts per chunk (pandas).
1. **Frequency-domain features** -- This stage apply Welch's method to extract dominant frequency bands from the acoustic chunks (scipy.signal).
1. **Adversarial validation** -- This stage train a classifier to distinguish train vs test data, and drop features that drastically differ between sets (LightGBM).
1. **Model training** -- This stage fit Support Vector Regressors (SVR), Kernel Ridge Regression, and LightGBM models.
1. **Stacking** -- This stage average the outputs and apply a strict floor/ceiling based on physical bounds of the earthquake cycle.

## Key Insight

The load-bearing idea is the pipeline topology: Signal chunking creates the representation needed by Stacking, so later stages can optimize the competition metric without solving raw-data ingestion from scratch.

## Failure Modes

- Distribution shift breaks handcrafted thresholds, post-processing rules, or validation-derived weights.
- Missing metadata prevents faithful construction of candidates, groups, windows, or cross-validation folds.
- A weak first-stage recall step cannot be recovered by downstream rescoring or ensembling.

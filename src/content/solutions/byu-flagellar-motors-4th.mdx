Object detection (2D) solution template for BYU Locating Bacterial Flagellar Motors - 4th Place (2025), modeled as a 6-stage deterministic pipeline: Data Ingestion, Medical Augmentation, Classification Feature Extraction, Object Detection, and downstream competition-specific post-processing.

## Approach

1. **Data Ingestion** -- This stage load cryo-ET biological imagery and systematically merge it with supplementary external datasets to increase diversity (pandas).
1. **Medical Augmentation** -- This stage apply domain-specific tensor augmentations (rotation, volumetric scaling, Gaussian noise injection) tailored specifically for biological and medical imaging (MONAI).
1. **Classification Feature Extraction** -- This stage train a straightforward 3D-ResNet18 classification backbone to simply identify whether regions contain flagellar motors, acting as a rapid filtration mechanism (torch.nn).
1. **Object Detection** -- This stage train specialized medical object detection networks strictly on the high-probability regions identified by the ResNet (MONAI).
1. **Model Export & Acceleration** -- This stage compile the trained models to TensorRT via TorchScript (JIT). This architectural choice enables ultra-fast inference, deliberately sacrificing a fraction of precision to allow for a vastly larger ensemble within strict competition time limits (tensorrt, torch.jit).
1. **Ensembling** -- This stage average the resulting bounding box coordinates and confidences across the parallel ResNet and MONAI detection streams (numpy).

## Key Insight

The load-bearing idea is the pipeline topology: Data Ingestion creates the representation needed by Ensembling, so later stages can optimize the competition metric without solving raw-data ingestion from scratch.

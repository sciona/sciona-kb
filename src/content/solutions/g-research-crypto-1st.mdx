# G-Research Crypto Forecasting - 1st Place (2022) - Pipeline Template

## Problem

Financial Time Series / Multi-asset regression solution template for G-Research Crypto Forecasting - 1st Place (2022), modeled as a 5-stage deterministic pipeline: Data alignment, Cross-asset features, Target neutralization, Group K-Fold training, and downstream competition-specific post-processing.

## Approach

This template captures the ordered steps used by G-Research Crypto Forecasting - 1st Place (2022): prepare the data, build task-specific representations, train or apply the main model, and convert outputs into the final competition submission.

## CDG Walkthrough

{/* <ScionaViewerPlaceholder cdgId="solution.kaggle.g_research_crypto_1st" readOnly={true} /> */}

1. **Data alignment** -- This stage join the 14 different crypto assets along the exact timestamp index, imputing missing minutes with forward-fill (pandas).
1. **Cross-asset features** -- This stage compute the ratio of every asset's volume/price relative to Bitcoin to capture broad market momentum (pandas).
1. **Target neutralization** -- This stage orthogonalize the training target against the general market movement so the model learns relative performance, not market direction (scipy/numpy).
1. **Group K-Fold training** -- This stage split data by time chunks and train LightGBM models independently per asset, plus one unified model across all assets.
1. **Post-processing** -- This stage apply the reverse mathematical transformation to un-neutralize the predictions before final submission.

## Key Insight

The load-bearing idea is the pipeline topology: Data alignment creates the representation needed by Post-processing, so later stages can optimize the competition metric without solving raw-data ingestion from scratch.

## Failure Modes

- Distribution shift breaks handcrafted thresholds, post-processing rules, or validation-derived weights.
- Missing metadata prevents faithful construction of candidates, groups, windows, or cross-validation folds.
- A weak first-stage recall step cannot be recovered by downstream rescoring or ensembling.

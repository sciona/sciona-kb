# Two Sigma: Using News to Predict Stock Movements - 1st Place (2019) - Pipeline Template

## Problem

NLP Sentiment + Tabular Time Series solution template for Two Sigma: Using News to Predict Stock Movements - 1st Place (2019), modeled as a 5-stage deterministic pipeline: News aggregation, Market feature engineering, Future leak prevention, Feature concatenation, and downstream competition-specific finalization.

## Approach

This template captures the ordered steps used by Two Sigma: Using News to Predict Stock Movements - 1st Place (2019): prepare the data, build task-specific representations, run the main modeling or search loop, and convert outputs into the final benchmark submission.

## CDG Walkthrough

{/* <ScionaViewerPlaceholder cdgId="solution.kaggle.two_sigma_news_stock_1st" readOnly={true} /> */}

1. **News aggregation** -- This stage group daily news articles by financial asset, calculating mean sentiment, article volume, and novelty metrics (pandas).
1. **Market feature engineering** -- This stage calculate rolling returns, MACD, and Bollinger Bands on the tabular market data (pandas/TA-Lib).
1. **Future leak prevention** -- This stage strictly align market data and news data ensuring news arriving after 10 PM is assigned to the next trading day to prevent look-ahead bias.
1. **Feature concatenation** -- This stage merge the aggregated NLP features and tabular technical indicators.
1. **Tree Modeling** -- This stage train LightGBM models. To prevent models from learning specific eras, train 10 separate models sequentially through time (Walk-Forward Validation) and average the result.

## Key Insight

The load-bearing idea is the pipeline topology: News aggregation creates the representation needed by Tree Modeling, so later stages optimize the metric without solving raw-data routing from scratch.

# H&M Personalized Fashion Recommendations - 1st Place (2022) - Pipeline Template

## Problem

Information Retrieval / Candidate generation solution template for H&M Personalized Fashion Recommendations - 1st Place (2022), modeled as a 5-stage deterministic pipeline: Trend generation, Collaborative filtering, Candidate merging, Feature engineering, and downstream competition-specific post-processing.

## Approach

This template captures the ordered steps used by H&M Personalized Fashion Recommendations - 1st Place (2022): prepare the data, build task-specific representations, train or apply the main model, and convert outputs into the final competition submission.

## CDG Walkthrough

{/* <ScionaViewerPlaceholder cdgId="solution.kaggle.handm_personalized_fashion_recommendations_1st" readOnly={true} /> */}

1. **Trend generation** -- This stage compute the top 100 most popular items in the trailing 2 weeks (pandas).
1. **Collaborative filtering** -- This stage use Alternating Least Squares (ALS) to generate matrix factorization candidates for users (implicit).
1. **Candidate merging** -- This stage union trend candidates, ALS candidates, and historically purchased items into a master pool.
1. **Feature engineering** -- This stage calculate user spending power, item price trends, and seasonal overlap (pandas).
1. **Ranking model** -- This stage train LightGBM in Ranker mode (Lambdarank) optimizing for MAP@12.

## Key Insight

The load-bearing idea is the pipeline topology: Trend generation creates the representation needed by Ranking model, so later stages can optimize the competition metric without solving raw-data ingestion from scratch.

## Failure Modes

- Distribution shift breaks handcrafted thresholds, post-processing rules, or validation-derived weights.
- Missing metadata prevents faithful construction of candidates, groups, windows, or cross-validation folds.
- A weak first-stage recall step cannot be recovered by downstream rescoring or ensembling.

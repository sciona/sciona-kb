# Deepfake Detection Challenge (DFDC) - 1st Place (2020) - Pipeline Template

## Problem

Video understanding / Image classification solution template for Deepfake Detection Challenge (DFDC) - 1st Place (2020), modeled as a 5-stage deterministic pipeline: Frame extraction, Face detection, Face alignment & cropping, Deep fake classification, and downstream competition-specific post-processing.

## Approach

This template captures the ordered steps used by Deepfake Detection Challenge (DFDC) - 1st Place (2020): prepare the data, build task-specific representations, train or apply the main model, and convert outputs into the final competition submission.

## CDG Walkthrough

{/* <ScionaViewerPlaceholder cdgId="solution.kaggle.dfdc_deepfake_detection" readOnly={true} /> */}

1. **Frame extraction** -- This stage sample 32 frames uniformly across each video file (OpenCV).
1. **Face detection** -- This stage run an MTCNN/RetinaFace detector on extracted frames to find bounding boxes (PyTorch).
1. **Face alignment & cropping** -- This stage apply margin expansions and align facial landmarks to a standard template, cropping to just the face (OpenCV/Albumentations).
1. **Deep fake classification** -- This stage train an ensemble of EfficientNet-B7 and Se-ResNeXt50 on the cropped faces with heavy image augmentations (PyTorch).
1. **Temporal aggregation** -- This stage average the frame-level deepfake probability predictions across the video to produce a single score (numpy).

## Key Insight

The load-bearing idea is the pipeline topology: Frame extraction creates the representation needed by Temporal aggregation, so later stages can optimize the competition metric without solving raw-data ingestion from scratch.

## Failure Modes

- Distribution shift breaks handcrafted thresholds, post-processing rules, or validation-derived weights.
- Missing metadata prevents faithful construction of candidates, groups, windows, or cross-validation folds.
- A weak first-stage recall step cannot be recovered by downstream rescoring or ensembling.

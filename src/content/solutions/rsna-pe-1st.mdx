# RSNA STR Pulmonary Embolism Detection - 1st Place (2020) - Pipeline Template

## Problem

Multi-task learning / Sequence classification (Medical) solution template for RSNA STR Pulmonary Embolism Detection - 1st Place (2020), modeled as a 5-stage deterministic pipeline: DICOM windowing, Spatial feature extraction, Sequence modeling, Multi-head output, and downstream competition-specific post-processing.

## Approach

This template captures the ordered steps used by RSNA STR Pulmonary Embolism Detection - 1st Place (2020): prepare the data, build task-specific representations, train or apply the main model, and convert outputs into the final competition submission.

## CDG Walkthrough

{/* <ScionaViewerPlaceholder cdgId="solution.kaggle.rsna_pe_1st" readOnly={true} /> */}

1. **DICOM windowing** -- This stage convert CT scan slices to 3-channel PNGs mapping different medical windows (lung, mediastinal, PE) to RGB channels (pydicom).
1. **Spatial feature extraction** -- This stage pass every CT slice through a 2D CNN (EfficientNet/ResNeXt) to extract embeddings (PyTorch).
1. **Sequence modeling** -- This stage pass the ordered sequence of embeddings from a single patient through a Bidirectional LSTM (PyTorch).
1. **Multi-head output** -- This stage project the LSTM hidden states to 9 distinct classification heads (slice-level PE, study-level severity, etc.).
1. **Post-processing logic** -- This stage apply rigid competition logic rules to the probabilities to ensure no contradictory predictions (e.g., cannot have severe PE if no PE exists) (numpy).

## Key Insight

The load-bearing idea is the pipeline topology: DICOM windowing creates the representation needed by Post-processing logic, so later stages can optimize the competition metric without solving raw-data ingestion from scratch.

## Failure Modes

- Distribution shift breaks handcrafted thresholds, post-processing rules, or validation-derived weights.
- Missing metadata prevents faithful construction of candidates, groups, windows, or cross-validation folds.
- A weak first-stage recall step cannot be recovered by downstream rescoring or ensembling.

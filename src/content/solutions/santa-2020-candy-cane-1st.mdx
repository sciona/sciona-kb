# Santa 2020 - The Candy Cane Contest - 1st Place (2020) - Pipeline Template

## Problem

Multi-Armed Bandit / Reinforcement Learning solution template for Santa 2020 - The Candy Cane Contest - 1st Place (2020), modeled as a 5-stage deterministic pipeline: Belief State Initialization, Opponent modeling, Bayesian UCB sampling, Action execution, and downstream competition-specific finalization.

## Approach

This template captures the ordered steps used by Santa 2020 - The Candy Cane Contest - 1st Place (2020): prepare the data, build task-specific representations, run the main modeling or search loop, and convert outputs into the final benchmark submission.

## CDG Walkthrough

{/* <ScionaViewerPlaceholder cdgId="solution.kaggle.santa_2020_candy_cane_1st" readOnly={true} /> */}

1. **Belief State Initialization** -- This stage initialize 100 distinct Beta distributions representing the unknown payout probabilities of the 100 bandit machines (scipy.stats).
1. **Opponent modeling** -- This stage algorithmically track the decay of machines specifically triggered by the opponent agent, updating the internal probability map without spending own turns (Python).
1. **Bayesian UCB sampling** -- This stage calculate the Upper Confidence Bound (UCB) mathematically to balance exploitation (pulling known good machines) and exploration (testing unknown machines).
1. **Action execution** -- This stage submit the highest-scoring machine index.
1. **Belief updating** -- This stage receive the reward (0 or 1) from the environment and update the Alpha/Beta parameters of the specific machine's distribution (Python).

## Key Insight

The load-bearing idea is the pipeline topology: Belief State Initialization creates the representation needed by Belief updating, so later stages optimize the metric without solving raw-data routing from scratch.

## Failure Modes

- Distribution shift breaks handcrafted thresholds, validation-derived weights, or post-processing rules.
- Missing metadata prevents faithful construction of candidates, groups, windows, graphs, or cross-validation folds.
- A weak first-stage recall, search, or representation step cannot be fully recovered by downstream rescoring or ensembling.

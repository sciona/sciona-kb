# SETI Breakthrough Listen - 1st Place (2021) - Pipeline Template

## Problem

Spectrogram classification (Anomaly detection) solution template for SETI Breakthrough Listen - 1st Place (2021), modeled as a 5-stage deterministic pipeline: Array formatting, Image augmentation, MixUp, Deep modeling, and downstream competition-specific post-processing.

## Approach

This template captures the ordered steps used by SETI Breakthrough Listen - 1st Place (2021): prepare the data, build task-specific representations, train or apply the main model, and convert outputs into the final competition submission.

## CDG Walkthrough

{/* <ScionaViewerPlaceholder cdgId="solution.kaggle.seti_breakthrough_listen_1st" readOnly={true} /> */}

1. **Array formatting** -- This stage reshape the raw signal arrays into 6-channel 2D spatial matrices representing different ON/OFF telescope positions (numpy).
1. **Image augmentation** -- This stage apply temporal masking, spatial masking (like SpecAugment), and horizontal flips (Albumentations).
1. **MixUp** -- This stage blend random pairs of 6-channel arrays during training to force the model to handle heavily overlapping signals (PyTorch).
1. **Deep modeling** -- This stage train EfficientNet-B1, B3, and NFNet architectures on the matrices using BCE loss.
1. **Multi-resolution ensembling** -- This stage average predictions of models trained at different image resolutions (e.g., 256x256, 512x512) to catch different signal frequencies.

## Key Insight

The load-bearing idea is the pipeline topology: Array formatting creates the representation needed by Multi-resolution ensembling, so later stages can optimize the competition metric without solving raw-data ingestion from scratch.

- A weak first-stage recall step cannot be recovered by downstream rescoring or ensembling.

# Connectomics 1st Place — Graph Inference from Calcium Fluorescence

## Problem

Unsupervised neural circuit reconstruction from calcium fluorescence time series via rank-reduced precision matrices with global burst deconfounding and hyperparameter-free threshold ensembling.

## Approach

Figure out which neurons are connected by looking at when they fire together, after removing spurious correlations caused by whole-brain activity bursts, and averaging over many sensitivity settings instead of picking one.

## CDG Walkthrough

{/* <ScionaViewerPlaceholder cdgId="solution.kaggle.connectomics_1st" readOnly={true} /> */}

1. **Calcium Lowpass Filter** -- Smooth the raw fluorescence signal to suppress single-frame noise, using several different smoothing windows that will be combined later.
1. **First Difference** -- Subtract each time step from the previous one to find moments when the signal increases rapidly.
1. **Fluorescence Hard Threshold** -- Discard small changes and keep only the sharp upward spikes that likely represent actual neural firing events.
1. **Global Activity Sample Reweighting** -- When many neurons fire at once (a network-wide burst), reduce the influence of those time steps because the co-firing is due to the burst, not direct connections. Amplify time steps where only a few neurons are active.
1. **PCA Precision Matrix** -- Compute which neurons are partially correlated (connected) by inverting the covariance matrix, using PCA to throw away the noisiest 20% of variation first so the inversion is stable.
1. **Score Matrix Normalization** -- Remove self-connection scores and rescale everything to a 0-to-1 range.
1. **Threshold Sweep Ensemble** -- Instead of guessing the best sensitivity setting, try 120 different settings and average all the results, which reliably outperforms picking any single one.
1. **Temporal Precedence Directivity** -- Detect which direction connections go by looking for a specific pattern: if neuron i fires at time t, neuron j should show a specific-sized increase at time t+1 if i connects to j.
1. **Weighted Score Combination** -- Blend the main connection-strength scores with a tiny amount of direction information to break ties.

## Key Insight

Burst deconfounding via nonlinear reweighting (x+1)^(1+1/S) suppresses global activity artifacts before precision matrix estimation. Dense threshold ensembling integrates out the threshold hyperparameter, equivalent to Bayesian model averaging under a uniform prior.

## Failure Modes

- Non-stationary time series where correlation structure changes over time: the single precision matrix averages over regimes
- Networks with hub nodes that participate in many bursts: reweighting may suppress genuine hub connections along with confounds
- Very sparse firing (most timesteps are zero for most neurons): covariance estimation is noisy even with threshold ensembling

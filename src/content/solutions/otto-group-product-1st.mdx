# Otto Group Product Classification - 1st Place (2015) - Pipeline Template

## Problem

Multi-label classification solution template for Otto Group Product Classification - 1st Place (2015), modeled as a 6-stage deterministic pipeline: Numerical Engineering, Interaction Features, Stratification, Base Modeling, and downstream competition-specific post-processing.

## Approach

This template captures the ordered steps used by Otto Group Product Classification - 1st Place (2015): prepare the data, build task-specific representations, train or apply the main model, and convert outputs into the final competition submission.

## CDG Walkthrough

{/* <ScionaViewerPlaceholder cdgId="solution.kaggle.otto_group_product_1st" readOnly={true} /> */}

1. **Numerical Engineering** -- This stage scale the base data and generate Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM) clustering assignments as entirely new synthetic features (sklearn.mixture).
1. **Interaction Features** -- This stage create hard-coded ratios, mathematical products, and statistical measures combining the original categorical and numerical columns (pandas).
1. **Stratification** -- This stage execute a Repeated Multilabel Stratified K-Fold cross-validation strategy specifically designed to perfectly preserve class distributions across all folds (iterative-stratification).
1. **Base Modeling** -- This stage train XGBoost and LightGBM models wrapped inside a MultiOutputClassifier to handle the diverse label space (sklearn.multioutput, xgboost, lightgbm).
1. **Level-2 Meta-Modeling** -- This stage extract the Out-of-Fold probabilities from the GBDTs and use them to train secondary Neural Networks and AdaBoost models (torch, sklearn.ensemble).
1. **Final Ensemble** -- This stage blend the Level-2 meta-predictions utilizing a carefully weighted geometric and arithmetic mean.

## Key Insight

The load-bearing idea is the pipeline topology: Numerical Engineering creates the representation needed by Final Ensemble, so later stages can optimize the competition metric without solving raw-data ingestion from scratch.

## Failure Modes

- Distribution shift breaks handcrafted thresholds, post-processing rules, or validation-derived weights.
- Missing metadata prevents faithful construction of candidates, groups, windows, or cross-validation folds.
- A weak first-stage recall step cannot be recovered by downstream rescoring or ensembling.

# HuBMAP Hacking the Human Vasculature - 1st Place (2023) - Pipeline Template

## Problem

Semantic/instance segmentation solution template for HuBMAP Hacking the Human Vasculature - 1st Place (2023), modeled as a 5-stage deterministic pipeline: Data Concatenation, Model Initialization, Weight Averaging, Mask Prediction, and downstream competition-specific post-processing.

## Approach

This template captures the ordered steps used by HuBMAP Hacking the Human Vasculature - 1st Place (2023): prepare the data, build task-specific representations, train or apply the main model, and convert outputs into the final competition submission.

## CDG Walkthrough

{/* <ScionaViewerPlaceholder cdgId="solution.kaggle.hubmap_vasculature_1st" readOnly={true} /> */}

1. **Data Concatenation** -- This stage merge multiple disparate origin datasets (dataset1 and dataset2) to maximize sample size and force the model to learn invariant representations (pandas).
1. **Model Initialization** -- This stage initialize an RTMDet-x baseline architecture tailored for instance segmentation over bounding box prediction (mmdetection).
1. **Weight Averaging** -- This stage apply Exponential Moving Average (EMA) to the model weights continuously during training. This technique acts as a regularizer and heavily stabilizes convergence in noisy medical imagery (torch.optim.swa_utils).
1. **Mask Prediction** -- This stage generate baseline mask predictions using the standard segmentation head operating on the spatial features.
1. **Post-Processing Dilation** -- This stage apply morphological dilation to the predicted binary masks to uniformly expand boundaries. This purely algorithmic stage significantly boosts the evaluation metric without requiring further network training (scipy.ndimage or cv2).

## Key Insight

The load-bearing idea is the pipeline topology: Data Concatenation creates the representation needed by Post-Processing Dilation, so later stages can optimize the competition metric without solving raw-data ingestion from scratch.

## Failure Modes

- Distribution shift breaks handcrafted thresholds, post-processing rules, or validation-derived weights.
- Missing metadata prevents faithful construction of candidates, groups, windows, or cross-validation folds.
- A weak first-stage recall step cannot be recovered by downstream rescoring or ensembling.

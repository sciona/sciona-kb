# Vesuvius Challenge 2023 - 1st Place (2023) - Pipeline Template

## Problem

3D Volume to 2D Segmentation solution template for Vesuvius Challenge 2023 - 1st Place (2023), modeled as a 5-stage deterministic pipeline: Z-axis slicing, Volumetric augmentation, 2.5D architecture, Decoder projection, and downstream competition-specific post-processing.

## Approach

This template captures the ordered steps used by Vesuvius Challenge 2023 - 1st Place (2023): prepare the data, build task-specific representations, train or apply the main model, and convert outputs into the final competition submission.

## CDG Walkthrough

{/* <ScionaViewerPlaceholder cdgId="solution.kaggle.vesuvius_ink_detection_1st" readOnly={true} /> */}

1. **Z-axis slicing** -- This stage extract sub-volumes around the known papyrus surface mesh (e.g., slices 20 to 40 out of 65) to discard empty air in the CT scans (numpy).
1. **Volumetric augmentation** -- This stage apply 3D rotations and random elastic deformations to the CT volumes (TorchIO).
1. **2.5D architecture** -- This stage treat the Z-axis of the 3D volume as the channels of a 2D image, and pass it through a ResNet34 backbone (PyTorch).
1. **Decoder projection** -- This stage pass the backbone embeddings through a UNet-style decoder to project the multi-channel volume down into a single 2D segmentation mask representing ink (segmentation_models_pytorch).
1. **Thresholding** -- This stage apply a strict probability threshold to generate the final binary map, dropping small isolated pixel noise.

## Key Insight

The load-bearing idea is the pipeline topology: Z-axis slicing creates the representation needed by Thresholding, so later stages can optimize the competition metric without solving raw-data ingestion from scratch.

## Failure Modes

- Distribution shift breaks handcrafted thresholds, post-processing rules, or validation-derived weights.
- Missing metadata prevents faithful construction of candidates, groups, windows, or cross-validation folds.
- A weak first-stage recall step cannot be recovered by downstream rescoring or ensembling.

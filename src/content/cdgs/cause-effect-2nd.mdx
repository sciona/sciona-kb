applicability:
  use_when:
    - Determining causal direction from bivariate observational data (two variables, no interventions)
    - Data pairs may be continuous, discrete, or mixed-type and the method must handle all three
    - The underlying causal mechanism is approximately deterministic (Y = f(X) + small noise)
    - Both independence detection and direction inference are needed (some pairs may be independent)
  do_not_use_when:
    - Multivariate causal discovery with >2 variables (this pipeline is strictly bivariate)
    - Confounders are likely present (IGCI assumes no hidden common cause)
    - The causal relationship is purely stochastic with no functional form (e.g., Y is noise given X)
    - Sample size is very small (<50 observations) — conditional noise statistics become unreliable
  key_insight: IGCI (information-geometric causal inference) exploits the independence of cause mechanism from cause distribution — a theoretical asymmetry that holds for deterministic causal systems. The cause's distribution and the mechanism mapping cause to effect are independently chosen by nature, creating a detectable statistical signature.
  critical_stages:
    - igci_asymmetry_score
    - asymmetric_feature_difference
    - two_stage_independence_direction
  swappable_stages:
    polyfit_residual_error: Any regression method (GP, kernel ridge, spline) can replace polynomial fits for residual-based asymmetry detection.
    weighted_ensemble_combination: Any ensemble strategy (stacking, simple average) works. The specific weights are competition-tuned.
    left_right_decomposed_prediction: Alternative estimator architectures (single multi-class, ordinal regression) can replace the left-right decomposition.
  failure_modes:
    - "Symmetric causal mechanisms where f and f^{-1} have identical complexity: IGCI score is near zero and direction is unresolvable"
    - "Heavy confounding: all asymmetric features measure spurious associations rather than causal direction"
    - "Highly nonlinear relationships with few samples: polynomial residual features underfit while conditional statistics have high variance"
  scaling_notes: ""

# Cause-Effect Pairs 2nd Place — Causal Direction from Bivariate Data

> For each pair of variables, compute features that are different depending on which causes which, then train classifiers on those features and enforce the physical constraint that if A causes B then B cannot cause A.

{/* <ScionaViewerPlaceholder cdgId="solution.kaggle.cause_effect_2nd" readOnly={true} /> */}

## When to Use This

- Determining causal direction from bivariate observational data (two variables, no interventions)
- Data pairs may be continuous, discrete, or mixed-type and the method must handle all three
- The underlying causal mechanism is approximately deterministic (Y = f(X) + small noise)
- Both independence detection and direction inference are needed (some pairs may be independent)

## Key Insight

IGCI (information-geometric causal inference) exploits the independence of cause mechanism from cause distribution — a theoretical asymmetry that holds for deterministic causal systems. The cause's distribution and the mechanism mapping cause to effect are independently chosen by nature, creating a detectable statistical signature.

## Pipeline Stages

### Discretize and Bin :white_check_mark:

Convert continuous values into discrete bins, ignoring extreme outliers so they don't distort the bin boundaries.

**Bound atom:** `sciona.atoms.causal_inference.feature_primitives.discretize_and_bin`

### IGCI Asymmetry Score :white_check_mark:

Measure whether the relationship from A to B looks simpler than the reverse, using a geometric information theory test.

**Bound atom:** `sciona.atoms.causal_inference.feature_primitives.igci_asymmetry_score`

### HSIC Independence Test :white_check_mark:

Test whether two variables are statistically related using a kernel-based measure that can detect nonlinear relationships.

**Bound atom:** `sciona.atoms.causal_inference.feature_primitives.hsic_independence_test`

### Normalized Error Probability :white_check_mark:

Measure how much knowing X helps predict Y, beyond what you could guess from Y alone.

**Bound atom:** `sciona.atoms.causal_inference.feature_primitives.normalized_error_probability`

### Uniform Divergence :white_check_mark:

Measure how far a variable's distribution is from perfectly uniform — cause variables tend to be more uniform than effect variables.

**Bound atom:** `sciona.atoms.causal_inference.feature_primitives.uniform_divergence`

### KNN Entropy Estimator :white_check_mark:

Estimate the randomness of a variable using nearest-neighbor distances.

**Bound atom:** `sciona.atoms.causal_inference.feature_primitives.knn_entropy_estimator`

### Polyfit Nonlinearity Asymmetry :white_check_mark:

Measure how nonlinear the relationship is by comparing a straight line fit to a curved one.

**Bound atom:** `sciona.atoms.causal_inference.feature_primitives.polyfit_nonlinearity_asymmetry`

### Polyfit Residual Error :white_check_mark:

Fit a polynomial from X to Y and measure how much error remains — the direction with less error is likely the causal direction.

**Bound atom:** `sciona.atoms.causal_inference.feature_primitives.polyfit_residual_error`

### Conditional Noise Entropy Variance :white_check_mark:

Check whether the randomness of Y is equally spread across all ranges of X — if so, X is more likely the cause.

**Bound atom:** `sciona.atoms.causal_inference.conditional_statistics.conditional_noise_entropy_variance`

### Conditional Noise Skewness Variance :white_check_mark:

Check whether the shape of Y's distribution changes across ranges of X — changes suggest X is the effect, not the cause.

**Bound atom:** `sciona.atoms.causal_inference.conditional_statistics.conditional_noise_skewness_variance`

### Conditional Noise Kurtosis Variance :white_check_mark:

Check whether the tail heaviness of Y changes across ranges of X.

**Bound atom:** `sciona.atoms.causal_inference.conditional_statistics.conditional_noise_kurtosis_variance`

### Conditional Distribution Similarity :white_check_mark:

Check whether the full shape of Y's distribution stays the same across ranges of X, not just the average or spread.

**Bound atom:** `sciona.atoms.causal_inference.conditional_statistics.conditional_distribution_similarity`

### Asymmetric Feature Difference :white_check_mark:

Compute every measurement both ways and subtract — the difference tells you which direction is more likely causal.

### Symmetrized Prediction Fusion :white_check_mark:

Force the final scores to be exactly opposite for A→B vs B→A.

**Bound atom:** `sciona.atoms.causal_inference.estimators.symmetrized_prediction_fusion`

### Two-Stage Independence+Direction Estimator :white_check_mark:

First decide if the variables are related at all, then decide which direction the relationship goes — don't try to assign direction to unrelated variables.

**Bound atom:** `sciona.atoms.causal_inference.estimators.two_stage_independence_direction`

### Left-Right Decomposed Estimator :white_check_mark:

Train two separate detectors — one for each direction — and take the difference.

**Bound atom:** `sciona.atoms.causal_inference.estimators.left_right_decomposed_prediction`

### Weighted Ensemble Combination :white_check_mark:

Blend three different classifiers with learned weights to get the final causal score.

**Bound atom:** `sciona.atoms.causal_inference.estimators.weighted_ensemble_combination`

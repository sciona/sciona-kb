# TrackML Particle Tracking 5th Place — Physics-Geometric Track Reconstruction

> Find which detector hits belong to the same particle track by fitting helical curves through groups of hits, using physics to predict where the next hit should be, and learning small corrections for real-world imperfections.

{/* <ScionaViewerPlaceholder cdgId="solution.kaggle.trackml_5th" readOnly={true} /> */}

## When to Use This

- Reconstructing trajectories from 3D point hits in a layered detector geometry with known magnetic field
- Trajectories follow approximately helical paths (charged particles in uniform B-field, orbits under gravity, etc.)
- Hit density is high enough that nearest-neighbor matching alone is ambiguous — physics-based prediction is needed
- Real-time or near-real-time processing is required (analytic intersections are O(1) per query, no iterative solvers)

## Key Insight

The Kepler-equation reduction for helix-point distance connects particle physics tracking to orbital mechanics, enabling O(1) nearest-point queries instead of iterative root-finding. Three hits uniquely determine a helix, and analytic cylinder/cap intersections predict next-layer hits without numerical integration.

## Pipeline Stages

### Detector Geometry Autodiscovery :white_check_mark:

Figure out the detector's layered structure automatically from the positions of its modules.

### Coordinate Rescaling for kNN :white_check_mark:

Warp the coordinate system so that nearby hits in the detector sense are also nearby in the math sense, enabling standard nearest-neighbor search.

**Bound atom:** `sciona.atoms.particle_tracking.detector_corrections.coordinate_rescaling_for_knn`

### Per-Layer kNN Search :white_check_mark:

For each detector layer, find the closest hits to a predicted position using a standard nearest-neighbor search.

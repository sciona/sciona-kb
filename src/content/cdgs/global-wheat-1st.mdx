# Global Wheat Detection 1st Place — Pseudo-Labeled Multi-Detector Fusion

> Detect wheat heads in field images by training two different detection models with aggressive image augmentation, using the models to generate labels on unlabeled test data, retraining on the combined data, and merging the bounding boxes from both models.

{/* <ScionaViewerPlaceholder cdgId="solution.kaggle.global_wheat_1st" readOnly={true} /> */}

## When to Use This

- Dense object detection with significant domain shift between training and test distributions (different geographies, lighting, sensors)
- Single-class or few-class detection of small, visually homogeneous objects where localization precision matters more than classification
- Unlabeled test data is available at training time, enabling semi-supervised pseudo-labeling
- Moderate dataset size (1K-10K images) where heavy augmentation and self-training provide meaningful generalization gains

## Key Insight

The pipeline's power comes from the synergy between architectural diversity and pseudo-label domain adaptation. Two architecturally different detectors (two-stage Faster R-CNN vs. one-stage YOLO) make complementary errors: Faster R-CNN has higher recall on occluded objects while YOLO is better at dense small objects. Pseudo-labeling bridges the train-test domain gap by letting the models adapt to test-time field conditions, and WBF exploits the complementary error profiles by averaging box coordinates weighted by confidence rather than simply suppressing duplicates.

## Pipeline Stages

### Mosaic Augmentation :warning:

Create more varied training images by stitching four images together, pasting random patches between images, and randomly shifting colors.

**Bound atom:** `sciona.atoms.dl.image_augmentation.cutmix_apply`

### Heavy Detector :warning:

Train a large, accurate detection model that first proposes candidate regions, then classifies and refines each one.

**Bound atom:** `sciona.atoms.dl.architectures.resnet_family_backbone`

### Light Detector :white_check_mark:

Train a fast, single-pass detection model that predicts all bounding boxes at once, providing a different perspective from the heavy model.

**Bound atom:** `sciona.atoms.dl.architectures.yolo_object_detector`

### Pseudo-Labeling :white_check_mark:

Use the trained models to predict wheat head locations on unlabeled images, keeping only the highly confident predictions as new training labels.

**Bound atom:** `sciona.atoms.ml.tabular.gradient_boosting.extract_pseudo_labels`

### Pseudo-Label Retraining :white_check_mark:

Retrain both detection models on the expanded dataset that now includes the auto-labeled test images, allowing the models to adapt to the test conditions.

**Bound atom:** `sciona.atoms.ml.sklearn.semi_supervised.self_training_fit`

### Weighted Box Fusion :white_check_mark:

Combine the bounding boxes from both models by averaging overlapping boxes weighted by their confidence, producing more accurate final boxes than simply picking one.

**Bound atom:** `sciona.atoms.dl.detection.wbf`

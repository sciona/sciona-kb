# Lyft Motion Prediction 1st Place — Multi-Modal Trajectory Forecasting from BEV Rasterization

> Predict where nearby cars will drive by converting road maps and vehicle histories into overhead images, feeding them through image-recognition neural networks that output three possible future paths with confidence scores.

{/* <ScionaViewerPlaceholder cdgId="solution.kaggle.lyft_motion_1st" readOnly={true} /> */}

## When to Use This

- Inputs are heterogeneous (vector maps, agent state histories, ego pose) and need to be fused into a uniform representation for a standard backbone
- The prediction target is inherently multi-modal — multiple distinct future outcomes are plausible (e.g., turn left vs. turn right vs. go straight)
- Pre-trained ImageNet backbones are available and the team wants to leverage transfer learning without designing a custom encoder
- Spatial context (road geometry, lane topology, relative agent positions) is critical to the prediction

## Key Insight

BEV rasterization converts heterogeneous inputs (vector maps, agent histories, ego pose) into a uniform image tensor where standard CNNs work out of the box. The multi-modal NLL loss handles the inherent multi-modality of future trajectories — the car could turn left OR right — by only penalizing the closest mode, preventing the mode-averaging problem that plagues single-output regression.

## Key Insight

The load-bearing idea is the pipeline topology: Z-axis slicing creates the representation needed by Thresholding, so later stages can optimize the competition metric without solving raw-data ingestion from scratch.

## Pipeline Stages

### Z-axis slicing :red_circle:

This stage extract sub-volumes around the known papyrus surface mesh (e.g., slices 20 to 40 out of 65) to discard empty air in the CT scans (numpy).

### Volumetric augmentation :red_circle:

This stage apply 3D rotations and random elastic deformations to the CT volumes (TorchIO).

### 2.5D architecture :red_circle:

This stage treat the Z-axis of the 3D volume as the channels of a 2D image, and pass it through a ResNet34 backbone (PyTorch).

### Decoder projection :red_circle:

This stage pass the backbone embeddings through a UNet-style decoder to project the multi-channel volume down into a single 2D segmentation mask representing ink (segmentation_models_pytorch).

### Thresholding :red_circle:

This stage apply a strict probability threshold to generate the final binary map, dropping small isolated pixel noise.

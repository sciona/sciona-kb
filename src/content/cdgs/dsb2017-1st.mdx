# DSB2017 1st Place — Lung Cancer Detection from CT Scans

> Find suspicious lumps in a 3D lung scan, crop and classify each one, then combine the per-lump cancer probabilities into a single patient-level prediction using a formula that accounts for the detector possibly missing some lumps entirely.

{/* <ScionaViewerPlaceholder cdgId="solution.kaggle.dsb2017_1st" readOnly={true} /> */}

## When to Use This

- Patient-level classification from 3D medical volumes where the signal comes from localized lesions (nodules, tumors, masses)
- The volume is too large for direct end-to-end classification — a detect-then-classify decomposition is needed
- The detector may miss relevant findings, and missed detections must be accounted for in aggregation (not just max/mean)
- Multiple instances (nodules) per patient with independent cancer probabilities that should be combined

## Key Insight

Noisy-OR with a learned baseline (initialized at sigmoid(-30) ~ 0) correctly models the case where ZERO detected nodules are cancerous but cancer exists in an undetected region. The baseline acts as a learned 'leak' probability that the detector missed something, starting near zero so the model initially relies on detected nodules and learns to increase the leak only if the detector systematically misses cancerous cases.
